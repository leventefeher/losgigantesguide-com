The most accessible walk from Los Gigantes. It follows the coast south from the marina along a concrete and stone path, past a series of natural rock pools and small coves, with the cliffs shrinking behind you as you go.

## The route

Start at the marina and follow the waterfront path heading south. The path is wide and flat for the first kilometre, then narrows into a rougher track over lava rock. The turnaround point is a small secluded beach at about 2km — you can swim here if the swell is calm.

## What to look out for

The rock pools hold starfish, small fish, and the occasional octopus. The water clarity here is exceptional — worth stopping to look down even if you're not swimming. On a clear morning you can see La Gomera from the southern tip of the path.

## Practical tips

- Best before 10am in summer — it gets exposed once the sun is up
- Wear shoes you don't mind getting wet near the rock pools
- The return is the same path; allow slightly longer heading back as it's uphill at the end
